Nanjing Culture and History

Ancient Capital of Six Dynasties, Thousand Years of Literature

Historical Timeline

229 A.D.

Wu Emperor Sun Quan built the capital Jianye

During the Three Kingdoms period, Sun Quan established the capital in Jianye，opening the history of Nanjing as a capital city.

1368 A.D

Zhu Yuanzhang established the Ming Dynasty

Ming Dynasty Emperor Zhu Yuanzhang claimed the throne and established the capital of Nanjing, creating the Ming Dynasty's heyday.

1912年

Republican period (1912-1949)

Nanjing became the capital of the Republic of China and witnessed an important moment in China's modern history.

Cultural Heritage

The Ming city wall

The world's longest surviving ancient city wall, an important cultural symbol of Nanjing.

Year of construction: Ming Dynasty (UNESCO) World Cultural Heritage

Porcelain Tower of Nanjing

Royal temple in Ming Dynasty, the glazed pagoda was once the symbol of Jinling.

Construction Year: Ming Yongle Period National Key Cultural Relics

Culture Activities

Qinhuai Lantern Festival

Held annually in the first month of the lunar calendar, it showcases the traditional cultural charm of the ancient city of Jinling.

Time: First month of the lunar calendar

Jinling Cultural Festival

Showcasing Nanjing's distinctive culture, including theatre, folklore, food and many other activities.

Holding time: Every September
